Are you familiar with the term “set-jetting”? “Set” refers to movie sets, or filming locations. “Jetting” refers to travel. So, set-jetting is to travel to places that you’ve seen in movies. This can be touring London in a high-speed boat like James Bond, or visiting the estates that are featured in film adaptations of Jane Austen novels. Gretchen Kelly, a New York journalist, first coined the term “set-jetting”.
The concept may be relatively new, but it’s rapidly becoming a major factor for travelers who aren’t too sure about where to go. Often when travelers see a place in a major movie, it may compel them to make an effort to visit the place, whether on family vacation, a honeymoon, or a backpacking trip. More examples of set-jetting are people visiting France after watching “The Da Vinci Code” and travelling to Thailand after seeing “The Beach”.

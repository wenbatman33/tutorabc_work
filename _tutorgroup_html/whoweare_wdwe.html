Why do we exist?

Most schools are built around teachers – not students.

Students learn what teachers decide they learn, and the time teachers decide it’s time to learn, with the material the teachers say is right.

You don’t need physical classrooms.

A student in Taiwan wants to learn English. A TESOL certified English language teacher in New York City is looking for a job. Why would these two need a classroom to aid the learning process?

The best teacher-student ratio isn’t 1:1.

Engagement and interaction is critical to the learning process—not just with teachers, but with other students.

You don’t need the same teacher day after day.

In the real world, you don’t speak to just one person every day. Learning language should be no different.

You don’t learn the same way over time.

Not all students learn the same. Your education should match your pace.

Learning is as much about failure as it is success.

“Practice” is the art of failing repeatedly until success is achieved. This should be embraced in education.

The school day isn’t practical.

Some students are early-risers, while others are night owls. Some students can only learn over lunch. When the student picks their preferred time, the best learning happens.
